Productivity tools

17th July 1991
1 minute read
Tweet

Here’s my deliberate efforts to stay productive and plan out my daily routines. These tools and techniques are based on my personal preference and what works for me my not work for you. Also, I may have literally let go of some of these, with of course, for employers and clientele preferences. For starters, you want to have a look at Scot Hanselman’s talk on Scaling Yourself.

Contents

  • Notes written in markdown using Paper app
  • Task Tracking using GitHub
  • Reminders and invites on a calendar
  • Emails are filtered using Outlook rules
  • Reading as in books are only on Audible
  • Budgets are planned monthly using Google Keep

I interact with GitHub daily so why not use it for everything else.

Notes

  • Written in Markdown using Paper app

Previously used OneNote, Evernote, Workflowy, Dropbox Paper, and even Notion.

Task Tracking

  • GitHub Issues for task and bug tracking
  • Milestones for road maps and sprint planning, and
  • Labels to categorize based on features, bugs, and whatnot

These are just to keep track of various projects and not to plan my daily routines. Previously used Trello, Todoist, and even Asana.

Reminders

  • Google Calendar

Previously tried Workflowy, Microsoft Todo, Trello, but it has always been a calendar when scheduling meetings, appointments, reminders et al. whether it’s personal or work.

Emails

  • Rules on Microsoft Outlook to filter and process emails and meeting invites

You want to have a look at Scott Hanselman’s article on Outlook rules for processing emails.

Reading

  • Audible app on the go

Credit goes to former colleague and a dear friend, @hasith, for showing how keep up using Audible as I’m a snail-paced reader, and still reads a chapter or two from books occationally to get inspiration for fiction writing.

Budgets

  • Google Keep as a simple list of to-dos with the amount

Previously tried many apps and even spreadsheets but Keep seemed to work consistantly more than a couple of years.
