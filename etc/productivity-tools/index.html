Productivity tools

17th July 1991
1 minute read
Tweet

Here’s my deliberate efforts to stay productive and plan out my days. These tools and techniques are based on my personal preference and what works for me my not work for you. Also, I may have literally let go of some of these, with of course, for employers and clientele preferences. For starters, you want to have a look at Scot Hanselman’s talk on Scaling Yourself.

Notes

I interact with GitHub daily so why not use it for everything else.

  • Written in Markdown using Paper app

Current employer use Sharepoint for various documentation and templates, and some of the former employers used Confluence and Google Sites. Same goes when it comes to clientele.

Task Tracking

Everything’s on GitHub:

  • GitHub Issues for task and bug tracking
  • Milestones for road maps and sprint planning, and
  • Labels to categorize based on features, bugs, and whatnot

Current employer has homegrown enterprise tools with Microsoft Project. Former employers had JIRA, and back in the days this is done using speadsheets. Current client uses Agile Boards on Azure and former clients also had JIRA and TFS.

Reminders

  • Google Calendar

It has always been a calendar to schedule meetings, appointments, reminders et al. whether it’s personal or work. Current and former employers and clientele have been either on Outlook or G-suite.

Emails

  • Rules on Microsoft Outlook to filter emails and meeting invites
